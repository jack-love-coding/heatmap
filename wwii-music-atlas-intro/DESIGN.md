## Style Prompt

Build a cinematic archive-interface trailer for WWII Music Atlas. The piece should feel like a dark museum control room where map evidence, radio sound, artist dossiers, and historical timelines move together. Use the existing app's visual language: deep blue-black canvas, ivory text, copper emphasis, muted country colors, serif headlines, compact sans interface labels, map grids, thin rules, archival image panels, and disciplined motion.

## Colors

- Background: `#070a0f`
- Surface: `#0d1218`
- Surface raised: `#111922`
- Text: `#efe4d0`
- Muted text: `#cfc0a8`
- Copper accent: `#c98f58`
- Jazz amber: `#d4a24b`
- Broadcast blue: `#5f86c9`
- Lyric teal: `#5f9b8f`
- State red: `#c95b4f`

## Typography

- Display: Georgia, "Noto Serif SC", "Times New Roman", serif
- Interface/body: "Segoe UI", "Microsoft YaHei", Arial, sans-serif
- Data: "Cascadia Mono", Consolas, monospace

## Motion

- Medium cinematic pacing with 0.5-0.7s overlaps between scenes.
- Primary transition: incoming scene appears through a clipped vertical reveal plus slight scale.
- Accent transition: staggered evidence panels slide in across the map.
- Ambient motion: slow screenshot drift, rotating map rings, pulsing timeline markers, finite waveform bar loops.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use neon, purple-blue gradients, decorative blobs, or generic tech UI.
- Do not treat the topic as a battle montage; keep the focus on music, archive evidence, and cultural movement.
- Do not crowd the frame with long paragraphs; each scene should read at a glance.
- Do not invent colors outside the project palette.
